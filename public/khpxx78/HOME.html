    Developing and Constructing the ICT Project
ICT4D (Information and Communications Technologies for Development)
  • is an initiative aimed at bridging the digital divide and aiding economic development by ensuring equitable access to up-to-date communications technologies.
  • refers to the application of ICTs toward social, economic, and political development with a particular emphasis on helping poor and marginalized people and communities.
  • ICT for development is a field of research and practice joining specialties from computer science, information systems and development studies.


    Types of IT Projects
Common IT development projects:
  • Web Development – aims to design, create, and launch a website.
  • Application Development – aims to create a customized program that addresses a specific need.
  • System Development – links together a number of applications that, when combined, may address a broader objective.
    • *No matter which type of IT project you are engaged in, you will observe that they follow a certain systematic pattern. This pattern is called a life cycle.*


    IT Project Life Cycle
  • One of the things that distinguish a project from activities that cannot be considered as projects.
  • As its name implies, an IT project life cycle describes the phases that a project goes through from its conception to its fulfillment.


    Typical IT Project Life Cycle
Phase 1: Problem Definition
  • This phase involves all the initial activities that will be needed for the project itself.
Phase 2: Planning and Designing
  • Prepares the essentials of the project even before execution.
    • Phase 3: Development
    • Usually where most of the work is done. The actual codes are put together by the developer, applications are integrated, and databases are created.
    Phase 4: Testing
    • A consolidated testing occurs after Phase 3 has been accomplished.
    Phase 5: Deployment
    • Once the system has been accepted by the stakeholders, the project is launched and exposed to the targeted users.
    Phase 6: Maintenance
    • After development, the need to maintain the delivered system arises. In this phase, the developers of the system should be able to provide technical support for the users of the system.
